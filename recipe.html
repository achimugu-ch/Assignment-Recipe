Naija Jollof

Naija Jollof (also known as Nigerian Jollof rice) is a beloved West African dish made with long-grain parboiled rice cooked in a rich tomato and pepper sauce, seasoned with spices like thyme, curry, and bay leaf. It's typically vibrant orange-red in color and often includes onions, garlic, and stock for depth of flavor.

Naija Jollof Rice Recipe

  1. Prep time: 20–30 minutes (chopping, blending peppers/tomatoes, seasoning meat if used)
  2. Cook time: 45–60 minutes (depends on rice type and meat, if included)
  3. Serves: 6–8 people
  4. Difficulty level: Beginner

Ingredients:

  • Long grain parboiled rice (3 cups)
  • Tomatoes, red bell pepper
  • Onion, garlic, ginger
  • Tomato paste (2 tbsp)
  • Vegetable oil
  • Curry, thyme, seasoning cubes, salt
  • Chicken stock or water
  • Optional: bay leaf, mixed veggies, meat/fish

Instructions:

  1. Blend & boil tomatoes, pepper, onion, garlic, and ginger into a thick sauce.
  2. Fry sliced onions, tomato paste, and the blended sauce in oil until it thickens.
  3. Season with curry, thyme, cubes, and salt. Add stock or water.
  4. Add rice, stir, and steam on low heat (covered tightly) for 25–30 minutes.
  5. Fluff the rice, add veggies if desired, and serve with protein of choice.
Summary tips

To make great jollof rice, use parboiled long grain rice, reduce the blended tomato mix, fry the sauce well, cook on low heat with a tight lid, avoid over-stirring, allow a slight burn for smoky flavor, and adjust seasoning to taste.
